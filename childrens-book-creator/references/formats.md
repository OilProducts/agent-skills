# Formats (typical ranges)

Use these as starting points, not rules. Match the user’s goals, attention span, and the story’s natural length.

## Board book (ages ~0–3)
- **Words:** ~0–200
- **Structure:** naming, routines, simple cause/effect, repetition
- **Notes:** every page turn should “reveal” something (even if tiny)

## Picture book (ages ~3–7)
- **Words:** often ~200–800
- **Pages:** commonly 32-page format in print; story often fits ~12–14 spreads
- **Structure:** tight arc, strong page turns, visual humor/meaning in art

## Early reader (ages ~5–8)
- **Words:** often ~800–3,000+
- **Structure:** episodic, short chapters/scenes, clear vocabulary control
- **Notes:** keep sentence structures simple and consistent; repeat key words

## Chapter book (ages ~7–10)
- **Words:** often ~4,000–12,000
- **Structure:** 8–15 chapters, steady momentum, light cliffhangers

## Middle grade (ages ~8–12)
- **Words:** often ~20,000–60,000+
- **Structure:** deeper character growth, richer subplots, larger stakes
